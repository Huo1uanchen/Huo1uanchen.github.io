SEX EDUCATION

A fascinating show. Far more than just sex education.

At 16, not yet an adult, but not just a child. In this somewhat confusing transition period, there is a lot of temptation and a hunger for the unknown. Sex education is not only about sex, but also about love, acceptance of self, friend ship, sense of responsibility and kindness to the outside world.

Although the theme is adolescence, it does not define the characters in this period of time as children who cannot make choices for themselves. Instead, it emphasizes how important “choices” are to the growth of a “person”. How important it is to learn how to protect yourself, how to please yourself, how to face your feelings. No one can stop a person who wants to be independent from himself, and no one can limit a brain that wants to think. The more we learn, the more we can see, the less we will regret the decisions we make. It is decision after decision that makes us who we are. “It’s all normal,” a phrase that has been used many times in the show has already told us that making wrong decisions or meeting problems are not that horrible. That is the key of our growth. What’s more, If we were all born differently, then our feelings about love and sex would be very different. So stop doubting yourself for being different from “someone else.” Respect yourself, find your own pace, and always make yourself happy for yourself.

I believe that this drama has changed many people’s altitude about sex. There should be enough openness and inclusiveness instead of evading when there is something related to this topic. It is really important for us, even the society to realize that there is nothing to be shame about sex.
